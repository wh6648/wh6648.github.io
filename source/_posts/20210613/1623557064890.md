On June 13, the NBA playoffs continued to fight fiercely. The Los Angeles Clippers beat the Utah Jazz 132-106, bringing the total score to 1-2. Mitchell contributed 30 points, 5 rebounds and 4 assists, but for the first time in two years, he suffered a single "zero seal". Even if he had a magic performance of 16 points in a single quarter, everything was in vain, and he sprained his ankle in the last quarter.

Mitchell averaged 28.5 points, 3 rebounds and 5.8 assists in the first round of the playoffs. Against the Clippers in the semi-final, Mitchell further upgraded, averaging 41 points, 3 rebounds and 4.5 assists.

Mitchell, who opened the chain, overturned the Clippers in a row, helping the Jazz take a 2-0 lead. Therefore, if the Clippers want to beat the jazz, they should first try to reduce Mitchell's score.

In the third game of the series, the clippers have been determined to trap Mitchell from the beginning. As long as the Jazz core catches the ball, it will be surrounded by two Clippers players, among whom Leonard is responsible for the trap.

In the first quarter, Mitchell made 0-for-3 shots, didn't score, only had 1 rebound, 2 assists and 1 steals. What's more, the Clippers took a 27-23 lead in the first quarter.

In particular, Mitchell has not scored in a single quarter for the first time since the playoffs in recent two years. The last time was the second quarter "zero seal" against the rockets on April 25, 2019.

Since the big trend of "being caught" is inevitable, and the Jazz are falling behind more and more, Mitchell can only let go.

A breakthrough stop in the shot hit, Mitchell officially opened his own open style scoring performance. After hitting a difficult three-point goal, Mitchell's hand completely recovered. In just six minutes, Mitchell scored 16 points in a row.

If only from Mitchell's data of 6-for-10 shots in the second quarter, with 16 points in a row, this is absolutely super level. However, the Jazz team in addition to him, the rest of the single quarter a total of only 10 points into the account¡° Mitchell didn't let the Jazz team mate "zhonglele", and the Jazz were 15 points behind at half-time.

After half-time, Mitchell chose a simpler, faster and more effective scoring method - pick and roll, then pull three points! After a wave of 13-5 small climax, the Jazz will narrow the 16 point gap to 8 points. However, after the suspension, Mitchell shot three times in a row, and also received a foul, after the physical decline of Mitchell was replaced.

In the last quarter, Mitchell scored twice in singles, and his personal score reached 30 points. However, when the game was still seven minutes away, Mitchell broke through the inside line to lay up. He accidentally sprained his ankle, and then limped off. Although he returned to the dressing room and came back to the bench, he did not appear again.
